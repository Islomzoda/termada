# Termada Build Week Video Identity

## Style Prompt

A precise operational control-room film built from the real Termada Mission
Control interface. The product remains the dominant visual: dense, legible,
local-first, and calm under risk. Motion behaves like an audited system state
transition rather than an advertisement. English narration and restrained
callouts guide attention without covering the evidence.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#0b0f14`
- Panel: `#111820`
- Primary text: `#eef2f6`
- Verified: `#3ecf8e`
- Approval: `#f2b84b`
- Evidence: `#5ed4d0`
- Muted text: `#93a1af`

## Typography

- Display and narration callouts: `Space Grotesk`, 700-900
- Commands, hashes, and evidence: `IBM Plex Mono`, 400-600
- Use tabular numbers for HTTP codes, timestamps, progress, and hashes.

## Motion

- Medium-energy push and focus transitions, 0.35-0.55 seconds.
- Interface captures use slow deterministic pans and restrained scale changes.
- Evidence enters in ordered steps; approval receives one deliberate pulse.
- Every scene enters; scene changes carry the exit. Only the final scene fades.

## What NOT to Do

- No gradients, glow orbs, bokeh, purple palettes, or decorative card grids.
- No fake terminal output, invented metrics, or simulated success claims.
- No text over the approval controls or verified evidence.
- No rapid glitch effects, jump cuts, or unreadably small captions.
- No music that competes with the English voiceover.
